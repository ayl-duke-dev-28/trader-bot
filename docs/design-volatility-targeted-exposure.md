# Design: Volatility-Targeted Exposure for Balanced Growth

## Problem Statement

The current strategy protects capital better than QQQ but gives up too much upside
in sustained bull markets. Over the saved five-year walk-forward period, the bot
produced an 11.04% CAGR with a -21.26% maximum drawdown, while QQQ participated
more fully in strong years but experienced a materially deeper drawdown. The goal
is balanced growth: increase CAGR without materially worsening the bot's current
drawdown profile.

Lowering the entry score from 0.55 to 0.50 was already rejected. It increased
trading but reduced CAGR, Sharpe, and drawdown quality. More activity is not the
objective; better deployment of existing capital is.

## What Makes This Cool

The bot gains a throttle instead of a binary gas pedal. It can deploy more capital
when QQQ is in an established uptrend and realized volatility is calm, then reduce
exposure as volatility rises. The strategy remains recognizable: the same signals,
benchmark core, stops, sector caps, and position limits continue to decide what the
bot owns. This feature changes how much total risk it takes.

## Constraints

- Paper-first. No live rollout before walk-forward and paper verification.
- Long-only and unlevered. New and pending buys must never take projected gross
  exposure above 95% of equity. Price drift above 95% triggers forced deleveraging
  toward the current target.
- Preserve the existing 5% maximum for active-sleeve positions and all sector
  caps. The QQQ benchmark core remains explicitly exempt from the 5% cap.
- Preserve the current 3% daily-loss kill switch, ATR stops, trailing exits, and
  cooldown behavior.
- Preserve the existing QQQ 200-day-SMA risk-off rule and its 20% gross cap.
- Use only information available before the trade date. A decision for session
  `t` must use one shared snapshot whose `as_of_session` is the last completed
  NYSE session before `t`.
- Live and backtest paths must produce the same decisions and order intents from
  identical snapshots. Fill prices cannot be identical, so execution and slippage
  remain adapter-specific.
- Do not optimize against one final-equity number. Evaluate return, drawdown,
  Sharpe, turnover, and stability across multiple periods.

## Premises

1. Balanced growth means raising CAGR while keeping maximum drawdown close to the
   current approximately 21% level.
2. The main performance gap is upside capture. The bot reduced losses in 2022 but
   lagged QQQ substantially during the 2023-2026 bull period.
3. Looser entry standards are not the answer; the 0.50 threshold backtest was worse.
4. Exposure should respond to observed risk, not forecasts of next-period returns.
5. Every candidate must pass point-in-time walk-forward tests with trading costs
   before it can affect paper orders.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Volatility-Targeted Exposure

Continuously scale risk-on gross exposure using trailing realized QQQ volatility,
while retaining the existing hard risk-off cap. This is the selected approach. It
is small enough to isolate in an A/B backtest and uses data the bot already fetches.

### Approach B: Ranked Momentum-Quality Portfolio

Rank the universe using multi-horizon momentum, relative strength, profitability,
and financial quality, then allocate only to the top names. This may add return but
requires trustworthy point-in-time fundamentals and has a much larger leakage and
implementation surface.

### Approach C: Regime-Specific Ensemble Weights

Change analyst weights across bull, bear, and rebound regimes. This could improve
signal fit but creates a high risk of fitting historical regimes instead of finding
a durable edge.

## Recommended Approach

Add a validated `risk.volatility_targeting` configuration block, an explicit
market-regime value, and a shared pure exposure/rebalance planner used by both
`RiskManager` and the historical simulator. Scope includes configuration loading,
the planner, live and backtest adapters, risk-state persistence, trade-log schema,
tests, and documentation. It does not include new alpha signals or data vendors.

### Configuration contract

```yaml
risk:
  volatility_targeting:
    enabled: false
    benchmark_symbol: QQQ
    lookback_days: 20
    target_annualized_vol: 0.18
    risk_on_min_gross_exposure: 0.60
    risk_on_max_gross_exposure: 0.95
    realized_vol_floor: 0.08
    realized_vol_ceiling: 0.60
    rebalance_band_pct: 0.05
    min_rebalance_dollars: 500
```

Validation requires a nonempty benchmark, an integer lookback of at least 10,
strictly positive finite target/floor/ceiling volatility, finite exposure values,
percentages inside `[0, 1]`, a risk-on maximum no greater than the hard 95% ceiling,
a minimum no greater than a maximum, the existing `risk.max_gross_exposure` inside
the risk-on range, a
volatility floor no greater than its ceiling, and a rebalance minimum no less than
zero. Invalid configuration prevents startup; it does not silently substitute a
different risk policy.

The research runner may override only `lookback_days` (`20` or `60`) and
`target_annualized_vol` (`0.16` or `0.18`). All other values stay frozen.
The formula uses the existing `risk.max_gross_exposure` as its anchor and the
existing `risk.market_regime.risk_off_max_gross_exposure` as its risk-off target.
The volatility block does not duplicate either setting.

### Point-in-time snapshot contract

Introduce a shared `DecisionSnapshot` containing:

- `trade_session` and `as_of_session`.
- Adjusted daily closes through `as_of_session`, with no forward fill.
- Current positions, equity, open orders, current broker marks, and signal scores.
- An explicit regime enum: `RISK_ON`, `RISK_OFF`, or `UNKNOWN`.

In live trading, `as_of_session` is obtained from Alpaca's exchange calendar in
`America/New_York`; it is the latest completed regular session before the current
trade session. In backtests, it is the immediately preceding session in the
simulation index. Weekends and exchange holidays therefore do not count as stale
calendar days.

The snapshot is valid only when QQQ's final unique bar equals `as_of_session` and
the volatility window contains every expected consecutive exchange session from
the Alpaca calendar. Duplicate dates keep the final observation after sorting.
Missing, non-positive, or non-finite closes invalidate the volatility input; gaps
inside the required window are not forward-filled. An invalid volatility input
returns a non-increasing fallback target with a reason code and suppresses core
replenishment and new buys for that cycle.

The SMA regime requires exactly 200 adjusted QQQ closes ending at
`as_of_session`. `close >= SMA200` is `RISK_ON`; `close < SMA200` is `RISK_OFF`.
Missing, gapped, non-positive, or non-finite regime history produces `UNKNOWN` and
`FALLBACK_REGIME`.

The same as-of boundary must cover volatility, the 200-day SMA regime, relative
strength, and scores. Correcting any existing date-`t` leakage is part of the shared
snapshot work and applies to both the rerun baseline and every candidate.

### Exposure formula

The first implementation will test a small pre-registered matrix rather than tune
dozens of combinations:

- Realized-volatility lookback: `20` or `60` trading days.
- Annualized target volatility: `16%` or `18%`.
- Normal gross anchor: the existing `80%` maximum.
- Risk-on gross floor: `60%`.
- Risk-on gross ceiling: `95%`.
- Risk-off gross ceiling: the existing `20%`.

Volatility uses exactly `lookback_days + 1` adjusted QQQ closes ending at
`as_of_session`. Returns are simple close-to-close percentage changes. Realized
volatility is the sample standard deviation (`ddof=1`) times `sqrt(252)`, then
clamped to `[0.08, 0.60]`. For a `RISK_ON` market:

```text
realized_vol = stdev(QQQ daily returns over lookback) * sqrt(252)
raw_target   = risk.max_gross_exposure * target_annualized_vol / realized_vol
gross_target = clamp(raw_target,
                     risk_on_min_gross_exposure,
                     risk_on_max_gross_exposure)
```

The planner returns the target plus one of: `VOL_SCALED`, `CLAMPED_MIN`,
`CLAMPED_MAX`, `FALLBACK_HISTORY`, `FALLBACK_STALE`, `FALLBACK_NONFINITE`, or
`FALLBACK_REGIME`, or `DISABLED`.

When the explicit regime is `RISK_OFF`, the existing risk-off behavior wins:

```text
gross_target = 0.20
QQQ core target = 0%
```

For invalid volatility or an `UNKNOWN` regime, the fallback target is the minimum
of the existing normal maximum, the last valid persisted target when available, and
current gross exposure. With no last valid target, use the minimum of the normal
maximum and current gross. The fallback therefore never releases new buy capacity.
`UNKNOWN` emits `FALLBACK_REGIME`. Regime is never inferred from the gross target. This requires changing the
core helper to accept the explicit enum, preventing a 60-79% risk-on target from
being misclassified as risk-off.

The QQQ core target remains 50% of equity in `RISK_ON` conditions. Extra capacity
above the current 80% cap therefore goes to the active sleeve, subject to the
existing 5% per-position cap, 20-position maximum, relative-strength gate, and
sector limits.

To isolate the exposure change, QQQ remains subject to the existing score,
ATR-stop, and trailing-stop exit rules in both the corrected baseline and candidate.
However, a QQQ exit is never canceled by same-cycle replenishment, and a stop
cooldown is honored. Changing those core exit semantics is a separate experiment.

### Rebalancing rules

- Calculate the target once per trading session from the shared prior-session
  snapshot. Hourly live cycles reuse the persisted result only when the trade
  session, as-of session, and configuration fingerprint all match.
- A target increase only creates capacity for normal signal-qualified buys. It does
  not buy weak names merely to reach the target.
- A target decrease blocks new buys immediately. Reducing-risk trims still run when
  the daily-loss kill switch blocks buys.
- Gross exposure is `sum(abs(position market value)) / current equity`. Pending buys
  reserve exposure using their remaining unfilled quantity. Pending or newly planned
  sells never release buy capacity until their fills appear in broker positions.
  New buys use `current gross + all remaining pending-buy notional` before receiving
  capacity and can never take projected gross above the lesser of the current target
  or the hard 95% ceiling.
- Open-order notional means remaining unfilled quantity, not original quantity. A
  limit order uses its limit price; a market order uses the current broker mark in
  live trading and the matching dated mark in deterministic replay. Filled quantity
  is already represented in current positions and is never counted twice.
- Consolidated sells include already-open broker sells and every newly planned
  higher-priority sell intent. Consolidate by symbol and cap combined remaining
  quantity at the currently held quantity. Pending sells suppress duplicate trims
  but do not release capacity. For marked exposure and trim planning, value remaining
  sells at the current mark, never at an above-market limit price.
- Intent order is: stop/trailing exits, portfolio-guard actions, score exits,
  risk-off core action, volatility trims, core replenishment, then new buys. The
  planner recomputes projected exposure after each higher-priority layer.
- A tripped portfolio drawdown guard is terminal for the cycle: after its liquidation
  plan, suppress volatility trims, core replenishment, and all new buys.
- Trigger trimming only when projected exposure is greater than
  `gross_target + 0.05`, or whenever projected exposure exceeds the hard 95%
  ceiling. Once triggered, trim toward `gross_target`, subject to whole-share and
  minimum-dollar tolerances. The turnover band never authorizes a new or pending buy
  above 95%; price drift above 95% forces a trim even when it is inside the band.
- In `RISK_OFF`, do not apply the 5-point turnover band: exposure above the existing
  risk-off target triggers trimming toward that target, subject only to execution
  tolerance. The 5-point band applies only in `RISK_ON`.
- Rank active positions by score ascending, then symbol ascending. A missing or
  non-finite score ranks as negative infinity. Fully liquidate in that order and
  partially trim only the final required position. QQQ is last and is trimmed only
  if active positions cannot reach the target or regime policy requires a smaller
  core.
- For a partial trim, calculate the required dollar reduction divided by current
  mark and round the sell quantity down to whole shares. Submit only when notional is
  at least `max(min_rebalance_dollars, current_share_price)`. A complete position
  close may bypass the $500 minimum because leaving it open would defeat the ranked
  liquidation plan. When no order qualifies, accepted residual exposure is less
  than `max(min_rebalance_dollars, one-share notional)`, logged once, and does not
  cause repeated hourly attempts.
  Example: a $620 required reduction at a $200 mark sells 3 shares ($600) and accepts
  the $20 residual.
- Existing open sell orders count toward projected exposure, and symbols with open
  sells receive no duplicate trim. Partial fills are reconciled from broker state on
  the next cycle.
- A higher-priority stop or score exit for QQQ suppresses core replenishment for the
  remainder of that trade session. The suppression session is persisted. On a later
  session, a still-nonpositive QQQ score creates another exit/suppression; a positive
  score permits normal core restoration. A stop cooldown is honored before later
  replenishment. This rule is applied to both corrected baseline and candidate paths.

Indicator and alpha inputs use the prior-session snapshot. Current-session broker
marks may trigger stops, value positions, and estimate order notional. They are not
fed into volatility, SMA, relative-strength, or signal calculations. Parity fixtures
therefore include both prior-session history and explicit current marks.

### Persistence and parity

Persist an atomic, versioned record alongside existing risk state:

```text
schema_version, trade_session, as_of_session, regime, realized_vol,
gross_target, reason_code, qqq_suppression_session, config_fingerprint,
calculated_at
```

Write by temporary file plus atomic rename. Reuse only when schema, session,
as-of session, and fingerprint match. Otherwise recompute. A read/write failure
falls back to the existing exposure policy for that cycle, logs the failure, and
never enables the 95% ceiling.

Golden-vector parity tests feed identical dated closes, current marks, positions,
equity, scores, open orders, and configuration to live and backtest adapters. They
must produce the same regime, target, reason code, projected exposure, and ordered
intents. Fill prices, slippage, and partial-fill timing are explicitly outside
decision-parity scope.

### Observability

Each cycle logs:

- QQQ realized volatility and lookback.
- Target volatility.
- Calculated gross target and the clamp or fallback reason.
- Current gross exposure.
- Any blocked buy or deleveraging trade attributable to volatility targeting.

The trade workbook action contract gains `REBALANCE`. Live and backtest paths use
that exact uppercase value. Its reason records pre-plan actual gross, projected gross
after pending/higher-priority sells, prior persisted target, new target, realized
volatility, reason code, and as-of session.

## Open Questions

- Whether 20-day or 60-day realized volatility wins the pre-registered discovery
  comparison. No additional lookback is introduced after results are visible.
- Whether a 16% or 18% target wins the pre-registered discovery comparison. No
  intermediate target is introduced after results are visible.
- Whether the current-symbol universe distorts long-history results. The A/B design
  uses identical symbols and reduces, but does not eliminate, survivorship and
  availability bias. A point-in-time constituent dataset is not currently available.
- Whether exposure can exceed 80% in paper mode after the historical gates pass.
  The feature remains disabled by default until paper verification is complete.

## Success Criteria

All comparisons must rerun the existing 80%-risk-on / 20%-risk-off baseline on the
same frozen data snapshot, dates, universe, costs, and walk-forward ML windows. The
baseline and candidates use the corrected shared as-of snapshot and planner ordering,
so the comparison does not preserve known date leakage or parity defects.

### Experimental protocol

- Discovery period: 2006-07-10 through 2018-12-31.
- Candidate-level withheld period: 2019-01-01 through the last date in the frozen
  snapshot. This is not a pristine holdout because observed 2023-2026 performance
  motivated the feature; it prevents direct parameter selection on that period but
  does not provide independent confirmation.
- Run the four pre-registered `(lookback, target_vol)` candidates only on discovery.
- A discovery candidate is eligible only if it has Sharpe no lower than baseline,
  maximum drawdown no more than 2 percentage points worse, and annualized turnover
  no more than 25% higher.
- Select among eligible discovery candidates by highest Sharpe, then highest CAGR,
  then lower turnover, then shorter lookback. Freeze that candidate before opening
  the holdout results.
- Evaluate only the frozen winner on the candidate-level withheld period. The full
  20-year curve and rolling windows are robustness reports, not additional
  parameter-selection inputs. Genuine confirmation comes from forward paper data or
  a future untouched period.
- Because the universe is not point-in-time, label all results with this limitation
  and run an added robustness comparison on symbols with uninterrupted data across
  each evaluation period. The feature cannot be described as free of survivorship
  bias.

Metrics are defined as follows:

- CAGR uses exact elapsed calendar days.
- Sharpe uses daily close-to-close returns, zero risk-free rate, sample standard
  deviation, and `sqrt(252)` annualization.
- Maximum drawdown uses the daily equity curve relative to its running peak.
- Annual turnover is total absolute traded notional divided by average daily equity,
  annualized by elapsed years.
- Rolling three-year windows start quarterly and require three complete years; they
  are diagnostic and do not vote independently as if non-overlapping samples.

The frozen candidate passes historical qualification only if it satisfies every
candidate-level withheld-period condition:

- Holdout CAGR exceeds the matching baseline by at least 0.75 percentage points.
- Holdout Sharpe is no lower than the matching baseline.
- Holdout maximum drawdown is no more than 2 percentage points worse than the
  matching baseline and never worse than -23.5%.
- At 15 bps, the candidate still exceeds baseline CAGR, has Sharpe no lower than
  baseline, and stays within the same drawdown gates. The 5 bps run must satisfy the
  full 0.75-point CAGR improvement.
- Holdout annual turnover and trade count increase by no more than 25%.
- A mandatory human review examines full-history and quarterly rolling-three-year
  diagnostics for repeated failure regimes. This is not an automatic metric and
  cannot turn a failed quantitative gate into a pass; it may only block enablement
  after the quantitative gates pass.
- No look-ahead, live/backtest parity, sizing, or risk-control regression test fails.

If no candidate passes every gate, the feature remains disabled and the existing
80%-risk-on / 20%-risk-off policy stays in production. A near miss is not rounded
into a win.

Historical qualification permits paper testing only. Default enablement requires
the forward paper period, deterministic scenario coverage, and explicit human
approval; retrospective results are never described as independent confirmation.
